                                  Digital Behavior & Mental Health Risk Dashboard
               Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing how digital behavior affects mental health. 
With an increasing screen time and online activity, it is important to understand how these habits influence anxiety, sleep, focus, and overall wellbeing.

I used PostgreSQL for data analysis and Power BI for visualization to explore patterns in user behavior and mental health indicators.

The goal of this project is:

To understand how digital habits impact mental health,
To identify high-risk behavior patterns,
To highlight key factors affecting wellbeing,
To support better lifestyle and mental health decisions

 Tools I Used:
PostgreSQL,
Power BI

Skills Demonstrated:
Data analysis with SQL (aggregation, filtering, CASE statements),
User segmentation,
Dashboard design and storytelling,
Health data interpretation

 Key Insights:
Screen Time vs Mental Health:
Higher screen time does not always mean higher anxiety, but higher usage combined with other factors can increase risk.

Lower Sleep Hours:
Users with lower sleep hours tend to show higher anxiety and lower wellbeing scores.

High-Risk Users Exist
A group of users show:
High screen time,
High anxiety,
Low wellbeing.
High risk users may need attention or behavioral changes.

Notifications & Focus:
Frequent notifications on digital apps can interrupt focus, but the impact depends on how users engage with their devices.

Digital Wellbeing Drivers:
Sleep and anxiety levels show stronger influence on wellbeing compared to screen time alone.

Dashboard Overview
The dashboard shows:
Average screen time by anxiety level,
Screen time vs sleep relationship,
Screen time vs anxiety,
Notifications vs focus score,
User risk segmentation (Low, Moderate, High Risk),
Wellbeing score distribution.

This helps to quickly understand:
How digital habits affect mental health,
Which users are at risk,
What behaviors need improvement.

Health Objective:
To understand how digital behavior impacts mental health and identify patterns that may lead to increased anxiety, poor sleep, and reduced wellbeing.

Recommendations:
There is a need to encourage healthier screen time habits,
Also promote better sleep routines willreduce low sleep habits,
Reducing unnecessary notifications to improve focus,
Raise awareness about digital wellbeing,
Identify and support high-risk individuals early by educating and creating awareness about the dangers or risk of high screen time.

Key Takeaway:
Mental health is not driven by one factor alone, from this research it is a combination of screen time, sleep, and digital behavior patterns. Small changes in daily habits can significantly improve overall wellbeing.
